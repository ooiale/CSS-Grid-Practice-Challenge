Easter Egg

Carnifex Maximus Vardorvis is a dead vampyre whose corpse is found in the Stranglewood Ritual Site. A high-ranking member of the Zarosian Empire, Vardorvis succumbed to the Strangler parasite and has remained under its control ever since, where the player encounters it during Desert Treasure II. A stronger variant of him can be fought again after the completion of the quest.

Vardorvis was a vampyre who served the Zarosian Empire under the rank of Carnifex Maximus, whose role was that of an executioner or headsman. He was one of four individuals entrusted with a medallion - the means to open the Ancient Vault where the Elder Horn was sealed - alongside Perseriya, Duke Sucellus, and the Whisperer, who were all high-ranking personnel in the Empire. At some point during the Third Age, after the Betrayal of Zaros, he was deployed to Lassar to defend the fortress from a siege by Saradominists. This included the Imcando dwarves, who pledged their allegiance to Saradomin in exchange for a new settlement after their previous home, Imcandoria, was destroyed. Once the Zarosian forces were driven out, the Imcando dwarves set to build their new city, naming it Camdozaal. During the siege, Yonrith Lovakengj, the opposing army leader, fled westward with his closest allies and settled in modern-day Lovakengj, which Yonrith founded. However, believed to be wounded by pride (as Yonrith was the first to escape him), Vardorvis brought a small force to pursue him all the way to the western continent, and made camp in what is now known as the Stranglewood. On the sixth day of their pursuit, Vardorvis' venators successfully located the Imcando dwarves. However, one of Vardorvis' subordinates, interested in studying the Strangler, accidentally infected him with a sample of the parasite on the same day. As a result, Vardorvis lost his vampyric immunity and was beheaded by the Imcando dwarves, who attacked their camp the next day. Despite having "died", the plague reanimated his remains and moved everything to the ritual site, where he would remain for thousands of years. During the Fifth Age, an adventurer who sought his medallion eventually found and apparently killed Vardorvis, who had remained under the Strangler's control, though it reanimated Vardorvis again shortly after their departure.
